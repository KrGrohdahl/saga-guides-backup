PUPPETRY GUIDE
Puppets and their usage, known as 'Kugutsu', refer to the unique subset of items that pertain to the creation, manipulation, and usage of puppets and marionettes, as well as the Specialization required to do so. When controlling a puppet, string techniques are not required to be made or used for a puppet of any category; it is assumed that the user's strings are of equal to one rank below the rank of the puppet for purposes of interaction. Additionally, these default chakra strings do not require chakra input. If these strings are cut, the user can choose to resume control of their puppets by expending chakra equal to the rank of the puppet, using the jutsu scale (+5 chakra per rank, starting at E for 5 chakra). The physical attributes of the puppet are determined by the puppet's rank. Basic strikes operate under normal basic strikes rules, dealing a maximum of one instance of damage per post at two ranks below the puppet's rank. This is not affected by Taijutsu or traits, and does not stack with the user's own basic strikes limitation.
PUPPET CATEGORIES
Puppets can fall into one of four categories: Traditional, Horde, Giant, and Human.
TRADITIONAL PUPPETS
Traditional Puppets are the most common and are the cheapest to produce. They offer the greatest diversity in their utility and design, and can utilize the highest number of modifications.
HORDE PUPPETS
Horde Puppets are similar to Traditional Puppets in their design, being that they are relatively simple and mundane. However, Horde Puppets are, as the name suggests, considerably more numerable. Horde puppets are numerous, individual puppets, comparable to Sasori's 100. Horde Puppets can have individual weapons, however they all use the same values. A puppeteer can only have one Horde Puppet out at a time and no other puppets alongside it.

GIANT PUPPETS
Giant Puppets are monstrous puppets nearly as large as Giant Summons in terms of raw size. Giant Puppets possess greater durability and greater physical attributes by default, allowing their basic strikes to deal instances of damage equal to the puppet's rank, while suffering fewer available mods. Giant Puppets must be a minimum of B-Rank. A puppeteer can only have one Giant Puppet out at a time and no other puppets alongside it.
HUMAN PUPPETS
Human Puppets are puppets made from the remains of a human being, hence the name, and enjoy special bonuses as a result. Human Puppets can be created from either another player's corpse or the corpse of an NPC, and are the most expensive due to their independent chakra pool from the puppeteer. Human Puppets can have two elements and two specializations each. Human Puppets can possess KKG/Bloodline Limits (EP Prize only) but cannot possess a summoning contract, even if the cadaver once did. Additionally, Human Puppets possess their own techniques; 1 of equal rank, 2 of one rank below, 4 of two ranks below, and 8 of three ranks below. They can possess S-Rank techniques, however these will count toward the Puppeteer's total limit, and they outright cannot possess Ultimate techniques. Human Puppets must be a minimum of B-Rank and always possess a flat 150 Chakra regardless of their rank. Puppeteers are limited to a maximum of 2 Human Puppets per thread, and can only have one active on the field at a time. When applying jutsu to a Human Puppet, all techniques do not need to be applied for at the same time as the puppet. If you are adding techniques to an existing human puppet, go through the Jutsu Registration process, then make a request to have those techniques added to the human puppet in your Item Registration.
MODIFICATIONS
Modifications, or 'Mods', are items equipped to the puppet to provide it with offensive or defensive abilities, or general utility. Ranging from weapons to armour plating to enabling flight, these modular additions to puppets are what make them shine. The amount of mods available to a puppet vary depending on its category and rank. Puppets do not have to be created with all mod slots occupied, and can add these on at a later, post-approval time. Modifications values vary based on rank and mirror jutsu; D to B-Rank get 1 Max Value while A-Rank and above receive 2. Unlike jutsu, Modifications cannot receive additional values through drawbacks and are limited to the amount given by their rank. You can find the preset values for Damage, Range, and Speed below:

MODIFICATION TYPES
Within the general category of 'Modifications', there are certain types that, due to their inherent utility or usefulness, have special rules, guidelines, or limitations applied to them. Rideable Mods are mods that can be worn like a shell around the user or mounted to be ridden, functioning as armour or a physical ability enhancement. This type of modification is applicable to puppets of at least A-Rank. Flight Mods are mods that enable puppets to fly, either without or without the user. This type of modification is applicable to puppets of at least A-Rank. Autonomous Control Mods are mods that allow the puppet to be controlled remotely, allowing the puppets to appear autonomous whilst still being under the control of the puppeteer, sparing it from the typical danger of having its chakra strings cut and immobilized. This type of modification is applicable to puppets of at least A-Rank. Durability Increase Mods are mods that increase how much damage a puppet can take before being destroyed. These mods can increase the durability of a puppet by allowing them to withstand 1 additional technique interaction, equal to the highest rank of damage that the puppet can withstand. Puppets can only have one Durability Increase Mod, and these mods decrease the puppet's speed by 1 rank. Additionally, these mods can have no other values.
DURABILITY
Puppets operate similarly to items when it comes to destruction and durability; they have a set amount of damage they can withstand before they break and are inoperable until repaired, or the thread ends. After a thread ends, puppets are considered repaired and anything lost is regained, as is also the case for items. However, the category a puppet falls into is the primary determining factor in its durability. Traditional Puppets can withstand two techniques of equal rank, one technique of the rank above, and any equal combination of lesser techniques up to one rank below. Traditional Puppets are undamaged by techniques of two ranks and below. Additionally, four basic strikes of one rank above or two basic strikes of two ranks higher will destroy individual horde puppets. Giant Puppets are destroyed by one technique of a rank higher, two of equal rank, and are undamaged by techniques two ranks and below. Giant Puppets are immune to basic strikes up to one rank above, but will be destroyed by two basic strikes of two ranks, or any number of basic strikes of ranks above beyond. Horde Puppets are destroyed by one technique of equal rank, two of one rank below, and are undamaged by techniques two ranks and below. Additionally, two basic strikes of equivalent rank or one basic strikes of a rank higher will destroy individual horde puppets. Human Puppets can withstand two techniques of a rank higher, or equivalent amounts of lesser instances. Additionally, six basic strikes of one rank above or three basic strikes of two ranks higher will destroy individual horde puppets.
